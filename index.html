Spatial Pattern Analysis

Discussion

This maps illustrate the temporal evolution of Hyderabad City's urban spatial patterns from 1990 to 2020, categorizing areas into "Scattered Settlement," "Sub Urban Fringe," "Secondary Core," and "Primary Core." Over this 30-year period, Hyderabad demonstrates a substantial and continuous urban expansion. In 1990, the "Primary Core" was relatively centralized, surrounded by distinct "Secondary Core" areas and a "Sub Urban Fringe." By 2000, both the "Primary" and "Secondary Cores" show significant growth and some outward spread. This trend intensifies in 2010, with the "Primary Core" expanding considerably and merging with several "Secondary Core" areas, indicating a more dense and integrated urban fabric. By 2020, the maps depict a much larger, more contiguous "Primary Core" that has absorbed many of the former "Secondary Core" areas, while the "Sub Urban Fringe" has been pushed further out, signifying extensive urban sprawl and the transformation of peripheral regions into integral parts of the burgeoning metropolis.
